## Starting point

A tax advisory firm with 40 employees processes a large volume of submitted receipts and documents from a wide range of clients every month. First-pass review — checking whether documents are complete and plausible before the actual professional work begins — was done entirely manually by qualified staff.

## Approach

Working with firm leadership, we built a workflow that automatically pre-processes submitted documents:

- relevant figures and receipt data are extracted in a structured way,
- checked against defined plausibility rules (e.g. completeness, obvious inconsistencies),
- unremarkable documents are cleared for regular processing,
- flagged or incomplete cases are specifically marked, with reasoning, and routed to the responsible staff.

One point mattered explicitly to the firm: **no automated professional judgment** — just a pre-sort that concentrates human review capacity on the cases that genuinely need it.

## Result

First-pass review of submitted documents now runs about 40% faster. Since unremarkable documents no longer require the same level of manual double-checking, available capacity for the actual professional review has increased noticeably — while maintaining the same rigor for cases that genuinely warrant a closer look.
