## Starting point

The sales team at a mid-sized machinery manufacturer received technical inquiries of varying complexity every day — by email, through a contact form, and occasionally still by phone. Every inquiry had to be manually reviewed, matched against an extensive product catalog, and turned into a custom quote.

As inquiry volume grew, the capacity of the two responsible sales engineers became the bottleneck — with a noticeable impact on response times to customers.

## Approach

Working with the sales team, we first analyzed typical inquiry patterns and identified the technical parameters that show up in nearly every request. Based on that, we built an AI agent that:

- automatically structures incoming inquiries (email and form),
- asks targeted follow-up questions for missing but necessary details,
- suggests matching products and configurations from the catalog,
- and drafts a complete quote, including pricing.

A human stays in charge at every step: every draft is reviewed before it's sent, and adjusted where needed. The agent handles the groundwork, not the decision.

## Result

Within six weeks of go-live, average quote processing time dropped by 65%. The sales team can now handle significantly more inquiries in the same amount of time — with quote quality holding steady or even improving, since errors from manual catalog lookups have been eliminated.
