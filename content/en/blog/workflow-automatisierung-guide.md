One of the most common questions clients ask us: "Wouldn't a no-code tool handle this?" The honest answer: often, yes — but not always. Here's how we make that call.

## When no-code is the right choice

No-code and low-code platforms are an excellent fit when:

- the process follows clearly defined rules (if X, then Y),
- the systems involved expose standard interfaces,
- requirements change rarely, and in small increments.

A typical example: automatically categorizing incoming emails and routing them to the right system. That's a clean, few-days build — no custom software development required.

## When custom development becomes necessary

No-code solutions consistently hit their limits at three points:

1. **Complex decision logic**: once an AI component needs to weigh context and decide autonomously, rigid rule chains stop being enough.
2. **Scale**: what works fine at ten transactions a day becomes a cost problem or a bottleneck at ten thousand.
3. **Deep integration**: when a process needs to be tightly woven into an internal database or a legacy system with no modern API.

## Our approach: hybrid, not dogma

In practice, the best solution is rarely purely one or the other. A typical setup in our projects combines:

- no-code/low-code for orchestrating simple, stable sub-steps,
- custom code for the AI-driven decision logic at the core,
- and a clean interface between the two, so each can evolve independently.

That avoids two costly mistakes at once: unnecessarily complex custom development where a standard tool would do — and a no-code construct that collapses the moment a real requirement shows up.
